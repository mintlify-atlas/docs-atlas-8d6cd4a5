---

Your interests are the core signal Quorent uses to decide what news to fetch. Every article in your feed and every story in your email digest is pulled from sources matching the topics you've selected. Keeping your interests focused and up to date ensures your briefings stay relevant to what actually matters to you.

## What interests control

Interests drive two things simultaneously: the **article feed** on your Dashboard and the content of your **daily email digest**. Quorent fetches new articles for your interests once per day. When you change your interests, those changes take effect at the next day's fetch cycle — your current feed reflects the interests that were active at the last fetch.

## How to update your interests

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    From your Dashboard, click the **Settings** button (gear icon) in the top-right corner of the header.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll to Your Interests">
    The **Your Interests** section is below the Account section. You'll see your current topic count shown as _N/10 selected_ at the bottom of the section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select or deselect preset topics">
    Click any preset topic chip to toggle it on or off. Selected topics are shown with a filled background. Unselected ones appear as outlined chips. You can select up to 10 topics in total across presets and custom topics combined.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a custom topic (optional)">
    Type any topic into the **custom topic input** field (up to 40 characters) and press **Enter** or click the **Add** button. Your custom topic appears as a filled chip below the input.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove a custom topic (optional)">
    Click the **×** button on any custom topic chip to remove it. To remove a preset topic, click it again to deselect it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save Changes** at the bottom of the page. The button label changes to **Saved ✓** to confirm the update was stored.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Preset topics

Quorent includes 15 built-in topic categories you can select with a single click:

- Artificial Intelligence
- Crypto & Web3
- Climate & Environment
- World Politics
- Finance & Markets
- Technology
- Science & Research
- Sports
- Entertainment
- Health & Medicine
- Business
- Space & Astronomy
- Startups
- Energy
- US Politics

## Custom topics

If none of the presets match what you follow, you can type any topic into the custom topic field. Custom topics have a 40-character limit. Examples: _Quantum computing_, _Formula 1_, _Nordic design_.

<Note>
  Custom topics are matched against news sources the same way presets are. Use specific, commonly covered topics for the best results.
</Note>

## Interest limit

You can have a maximum of **10 interests** active at a time (presets and custom topics combined). Once you reach the limit, all unselected chips and the custom topic input are disabled until you remove one or more existing interests. The current count is always shown as _N/10 selected_ in the section footer.

## Tips for getting the best results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Prefer specific topics over broad ones">
    _Artificial Intelligence_ returns more targeted articles than _Technology_ alone. If you want coverage of a subfield, add it as a custom topic rather than relying on a broad preset to cover it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Mix preset and custom topics strategically">
    Use presets for high-level areas you want broad coverage of, and save your custom topic slots for niche subjects you follow closely. This balances volume with specificity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rotate interests for a topic you follow occasionally">
    Because you can only have 10 interests active, consider swapping in a topic for a week when a particular story or event is happening, then swapping it back out.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Changes take effect the next day">
    Quorent fetches articles once per day. If you update your interests today, the new topics will appear in tomorrow's article feed and digest. Your existing articles are not removed — new ones are added based on the updated interests.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

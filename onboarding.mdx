---
title: "Set up your Quorent interests and digest"
description: "A step-by-step walkthrough of Quorent's onboarding page — choosing topics, adding custom interests, and setting your daily digest time and timezone."
---

The Quorent onboarding page is a single, focused setup screen that you complete once after signing in for the first time. It takes less than two minutes and determines what you read every day: the topics you select shape your article feed, and the delivery settings control when your AI digest arrives. You can update everything later from your dashboard, but it's worth spending a moment to get the initial setup right.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select your topics">
    The onboarding page displays 15 preset topics as clickable chips:

    - Artificial Intelligence
    - Crypto & Web3
    - Climate & Environment
    - World Politics
    - Finance & Markets
    - Technology
    - Science & Research
    - Sports
    - Entertainment
    - Health & Medicine
    - Business
    - Space & Astronomy
    - Startups
    - Energy
    - US Politics

    Click a chip to select it — it fills in to show it's active. Click it again to deselect. You can choose **up to 10 topics** in total. Once you reach the limit, unselected chips become disabled until you remove one of your current selections.

    A counter at the bottom of the page shows how many topics you've selected out of 10.

    <Tip>
      A mix of broad and specific topics gives you the most useful digest. For example, pairing "Technology" with "Quantum computing" (a custom topic) captures both mainstream tech news and specialized coverage.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add custom topics">
    If none of the preset chips covers a topic you follow, you can add your own. In the **Or add your own** field, type a topic name (up to 40 characters) and press **Add** or hit Enter. Your custom topic appears as a filled chip below the input field.

    To remove a custom topic, click the **×** on its chip.

    Custom topics count toward the 10-topic limit, so the **Add** button and input field become disabled when you've reached the maximum.

    <Info>
      Custom topics work the same as preset topics — Quorent fetches articles matching your custom terms from its news sources and includes them in your daily digest.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your digest time and timezone">
    Under **Delivery preferences**, two fields control when your daily briefing arrives:

    **Digest time** — Use the time picker to choose the hour and minute you want your digest delivered. The default is 07:00.

    **Time zone** — Select your timezone from the dropdown. Quorent automatically detects your local timezone and pre-selects it. The full list covers UTC, US time zones (Eastern, Central, Mountain, Pacific), São Paulo, London, Paris/Berlin, Moscow, Dubai, India (IST), Singapore, Tokyo, Shanghai, Sydney, and Auckland.

    If your timezone isn't listed, select the closest UTC offset manually.

    <Warning>
      Make sure your timezone is set correctly before completing onboarding. If you receive your digest at an unexpected time, check this setting first — you can update it later from **Configuration → Digest settings**.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and go to your dashboard">
    When you've selected at least one topic and confirmed your delivery settings, click **Start My Digest**. Quorent saves your preferences to your account and redirects you to the dashboard.

    From this point, Quorent begins building your article feed based on your chosen topics. Your first digest will be delivered at your scheduled time on the following day.

    If saving fails — for example, due to a network error — an error message appears above the button. The page retains all your selections, so you can retry without starting over.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## After onboarding

Once you've completed onboarding, you can update any of these settings at any time:

- **Topics** — Add or remove interests from **Configuration → Interests** in your dashboard.
- **Digest time and timezone** — Adjust your delivery schedule from **Configuration → Digest settings**.

Changes to your topics take effect with the next day's article fetch. Changes to your delivery time take effect for the next scheduled digest.
